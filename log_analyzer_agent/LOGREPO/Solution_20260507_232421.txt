### Incident Resolution Guide

#### Introduction
This incident resolution guide is created in response to a critical failure of the GraphQL API, resulting from the unavailability of the OrderService and InventoryService. The guide provides immediate, short-term, and long-term fixes to address the issue and prevent future occurrences.

#### Immediate Fix (What to Do Right Now)

1. **Manually reset circuit breakers**: Immediately reset the circuit breakers for OrderService and InventoryService to allow requests to be processed again. This is a temporary measure to restore partial functionality.
2. **Verify service responsiveness**: Check if the OrderService and InventoryService are responding to requests after the circuit breaker reset. If they are still unresponsive, proceed to the short-term fixes.
3. **Activate monitoring and logging**: Ensure that monitoring tools and logging mechanisms are capturing all relevant data to aid in the ongoing analysis and debugging of the issue.

#### Short Term Fix (Next 24 Hours)

1. **Investigate service failures**: Conduct a thorough investigation into the causes of the OrderService and InventoryService failures. This includes reviewing logs, checking for recent code changes, and verifying the health of underlying infrastructure.
2. **Implement temporary workarounds**: If the root cause of the service failures cannot be immediately identified or fixed, implement temporary workarounds. This could include reducing the load on the services, temporarily increasing resources, or redirecting requests to alternative services if available.
3. **Enhance retry mechanisms**: Temporarily adjust retry mechanisms to be more aggressive (e.g., increase the number of retries or reduce the time between retries) to help mitigate the impact of service failures on the GraphQL API.
4. **Closely monitor system performance**: Continuously monitor the system's performance and the behavior of the OrderService and InventoryService. Be prepared to intervene manually if necessary to prevent further degradation.

#### Long Term Fix (Permanent Solution)

1. **Address underlying service issues**: Based on the investigation, address the underlying causes of the OrderService and InventoryService failures. This may involve fixing code bugs, optimizing database queries, increasing service resources, or improving network configurations.
2. **Implement robust service monitoring and alerting**: Develop and deploy more robust monitoring and alerting systems for the OrderService and InventoryService. This should include proactive checks for service health, performance metrics, and automated alerts for potential issues before they escalate into critical failures.
3. **Optimize circuit breaker configurations**: Review and optimize circuit breaker configurations to strike a better balance between preventing cascading failures and allowing legitimate requests to succeed. This may involve adjusting the threshold for triggering circuit breakers or implementing more sophisticated logic for handling retries and service failures.
4. **Refine retry mechanisms**: Refine the retry mechanisms to be more efficient and less prone to exhausting all attempts too quickly. Consider implementing exponential backoff strategies for retries or using more advanced algorithms for determining when to retry requests.
5. **Conduct load testing and stress testing**: Perform comprehensive load testing and stress testing on the OrderService and InventoryService to identify and address any scalability issues or bottlenecks that could lead to future failures under heavy load.

#### How to Prevent This in Future

1. **Regularly review and improve monitoring and alerting systems**: Ensure that monitoring and alerting systems are regularly reviewed and updated to reflect changes in the application and its services. This includes adding new metrics, adjusting thresholds, and ensuring that alerts are relevant and actionable.
2. **Implement continuous integration and continuous deployment (CI/CD)**: Adopt CI/CD practices to ensure that code changes are thoroughly tested, including performance and stress testing, before being deployed to production. This can help catch potential issues before they reach production.
3. **Conduct regular security audits and penetration testing**: Regularly conduct security audits and penetration testing to identify vulnerabilities in the system and address them before they can be exploited.
4. **Develop and practice incident response plans**: Develop comprehensive incident response plans and practice them regularly. This ensures that the team is prepared to respond quickly and effectively in the event of a critical failure.
5. **Foster a culture of continuous improvement**: Encourage a culture within the development and operations teams that emphasizes continuous improvement, learning from failures, and proactively addressing potential issues before they become critical.

By following this incident resolution guide, the immediate issues can be addressed, and long-term fixes can be implemented to prevent similar incidents from occurring in the future. Continuous improvement and a proactive approach to monitoring and maintenance are key to ensuring the reliability and performance of the GraphQL API and its dependent services.
